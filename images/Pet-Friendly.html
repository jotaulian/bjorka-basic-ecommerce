Pet and Kid-Friendly Furniture and Fabrics

Our durable kid-friendly and pet-friendly fabrics are perfect options for your family's high-traffic furniture, from sofas to loveseats.
What's kid and pet-friendly about our furniture and fabrics?
We believe you can have your cake and eat it too. Right on the sofa, if you like. That's why we created a huge selection custom kid-friendly and pet-friendly furniture options that are hard-wearing, easy-to-clean fabrics and as stunning as they are strong. Although we're quite proud of the durability of all our upholstery, we have some standouts that are especially resilient.
Designed for the most active of homes, these pet and kid-friendly fabrics don't flinch at the sight of crumbs, fur, spills and stains. However, don't let that toughness fool you. They're still the soft, cozy upholstery your shedding pets, sticky children, and clutziest houseguests deserve to lounge on. And, we offer them in an array of colors to match your wildest decorating ideas.
So, go ahead and get creative. While we have yet to create a completely indestructible fabric for couches or loveseats, these pet and kid-friendly options are your best bet for helping your furniture hold up extraordinarily well under pressure. We understand that life gets messy sometimes. But that doesn't mean you can't have nice things.

What are pet-friendly and kid-friendly furniture fabrics?

To be considered pet-friendly or kid-friendly, furniture and fabrics need to be durable, easy to clean, and able to handle high-traffic situations in places like a living or family room. Some of our pet-friendly fabrics are woven, which means they're easier to clean or wipe down and won't trap pet hair. Pet and kid-friendly decorating tips

Match your fabric to your pet’s fur

Try to choose a fabric that’s similar in color to your pet’s fur. This will help hide hair between vacuumings - and help your pet go incognito.

Avoid light fabrics

No matter what, light fabric will always show marks and stains faster, especially in high-traffic areas. Opt for a darker color instead.

Skip or remove carpet

If possible, ditch carpet and rugs (particularly wall-to-wall), since they’re difficult to keep clean, and trap pet hair, which causes odors.
